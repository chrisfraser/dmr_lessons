DMR Radio Basics Lesson 04

The Voice Path & Transcoding

DMR speaks AMBE+2; your PoC platform speaks something else. The single most consequential decision in your bridge is whether those two ever have to translate.

You now know how DMR addresses voice (Lesson 3). This lesson follows the voice samples themselves through the bridge — because the format the DMR module hands your Android app decides whether your product sounds clean or muddy. This is the lesson to get right.

What DMR voice actually is

The vocoder is DVSI AMBE+2. It packs speech into a 30 ms frame of 216 bits — that's 2,450 bps of voice plus forward error correction, totalling 3,600 bps.1 Each frame decodes back to three 20 ms chunks of ordinary PCM audio.2 Two things follow: AMBE+2 is proprietary (licensed from DVSI), and it's lossy — it throws away detail to hit that bitrate.

Transcoding — and why it hurts

Transcoding means decoding one codec to PCM and re-encoding into another (AMBE+2 → your PoC codec, e.g. Opus/AMR). Because each codec is independently lossy, chaining them compounds the damage. DVSI's own testing found transcoding cost >12% intelligibility versus the same codec end-to-end, and they warn it should be avoided where possible.3 Worse, the loss cascades: every extra transcode in the chain degrades it further.4

The mental model Transcoding is like photocopying a photocopy. One copy is readable; copy the copy, then copy that, and detail smears. Each codec hop is a copy.

The fork in your bridge: relay or transcode?

Everything hinges on what the DMR module exposes to your app at the bridge point.

Why this is yours to decide You own the custom PoC platform — so you get to choose its codec. That's leverage no commercial gateway has. If you make the PoC side carry AMBE+2 end-to-end, you can relay and dodge the 12% loss entirely (at the cost of an AMBE licence on the PoC side and a codec most cellular stacks don't natively favour). If you'd rather use a cellular-friendly codec, you accept one transcode — keep it to exactly one hop, never a chain. The fork is set by one fact: does the DMR module hand you PCM or raw AMBE frames? Find that out from the vendor SDK first.

Latency budget

PoC adds cellular/IP round-trip delay on top of DMR's own framing. Every transcode adds a decode+encode pass. For a push-to-talk feel, budget the whole chain — DMR air framing (30 ms frames) + module processing + transcode (if any) + PoC network — and test it as a round trip, not per-stage. Relaying raw frames removes the transcode term from that budget entirely.
